# 3 · Open the Enclosure Without Breaking It

GEVINO boards (Opto PNP, Civile, Motor) ship in a **modular DIN-rail plastic enclosure**. You only
need to open it to **reach the micro-SD card**, change a configuration jumper/DIP switch, or fit an
optional module. The case is held by snap-fit plastic hooks — a few of them are **tiny and easy to
snap**, so follow the order below.

> ⚠️ **The two small hooks near the SD area are the fragile ones.** They break easily — even while
> you slide the SD card in. Take your time and never force the cover.
>
> This guide reflects GEVA's *How to open GEVINO Opto* instructions. The TFT 7"/10" uses a different,
> panel-mount housing — see its repository for panel-mounting details.

---

## Before you start

- **Power off** the GEVINO and disconnect it from the mains / power supply.
- On the **Civile**, if an internal 220 VAC PSU is fitted, make sure it is fully disconnected.
- Work on a clean, flat surface. No special tools are required; a small flat screwdriver only helps
  to *gently* nudge a hook — never to pry hard.

---

## The hooks

The cover is retained by three groups of hooks:

| Hook | Where | Action |
|------|-------|--------|
| **Red hook** | DIN-rail latch on the back | **Remove / release first.** |
| **4 main hooks** | Sides of the enclosure | Open these to free the cover. |
| **2 small hooks** | Near the SD-card opening | **Save them** — do not break. Fragile even during card insertion. |

---

## Opening — step by step

1. **Release the red DIN-rail hook.** It is the rail-mount latch on the back of the case. Disengage
   it first so the body is free to handle.
2. **Open the 4 main hooks.** These are on the sides of the enclosure and hold the cover to the
   body. Release them one at a time, working evenly around the case. Don't lever the cover up while
   a hook is still engaged — release the hook, then lift.
3. **Protect the 2 small hooks.** As the cover comes away, the two tiny hooks near the SD-card area
   are exposed. **Do not snap them off.** They are the most fragile part of the whole enclosure.
4. **Lift the cover straight off.** Once all hooks are released, the cover separates without force.
   If it resists, stop — a hook is still engaged.

---

## Inserting / removing the micro-SD card safely

The two small hooks sit right by the SD slot, so they are at risk exactly when you handle the card.

> 🛈 **GEVA's tip:** *to avoid breaking the tiny hooks — even during the insertion of the card —
> gently fold the wall outwards.*

- **Flex the wall outward**, just enough to clear the card, then slide the micro-SD in or out
  **straight** with light pressure.
- Never push the card in at an angle, and never force it against the small hooks.
- Most GEVINO models use a **push-push or push-in micro-SD holder** — insert until it seats; pull
  straight out to remove. Don't twist.

---

## Closing the enclosure

1. Make sure the **2 small hooks** are intact and correctly positioned.
2. Lower the cover **straight down** onto the body — do not slide it sideways across the hooks.
3. Press gently until the **4 main hooks** click home, one by one.
4. **Re-engage the red DIN-rail hook.**
5. Give the case a light squeeze all round to confirm every hook has clicked. The cover should sit
   flush with no gaps.

---

## If a small hook breaks

It is the most common mishap and it is **not fatal**: the cover is still retained by the 4 main
hooks and the DIN-rail latch. The board keeps working normally. If you want it repaired or a
replacement cover, contact GEVA.
